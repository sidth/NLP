June is the sixth month of the year.
The Latin name for June is Junius. Ovid offers multiple etymologies for the name in the Fasti, a poem about the Roman calendar. It is that the name is named after the Roman goddess Juno. June is the sixth month of the year. 
Certain meteor showers take place in June. The Arietids takes place May 22 to July 2 each year, and peaks on June 7. The Beta Taurids June 5 to July 18. June's birthstones are pearl, alexandrite and moonstone. June is the sixth month in the Julian and Gregorian calendars.
At the start of June, the sun rises in the constellation of Taurus; at the end of June, the sun rises in the constellation of Gemini.
The zodiac signs for the month of June are Gemini (until June 20) and Cancer (from June 21 onwards).
n the Northern hemisphere, the beginning of the traditional astronomical summer is 21 June (meteorological summer begins on 1 June). In the Southern hemisphere, meteorological winter begins on 1 June.
